Tool Tagging Tips


This page gives some tips (the dos and the don'ts) on how to affix markers onto the tools.

  • We see in the image below that in (a), two same markers are used. One is affixed to the handle of the tool and is hence curved, while the other marker lays flat on the ground. (b) shows that the curved marker isn't being detected. Hence, curved markers are not a good idea for accurate detections.


  • The image below shows two hammers with different marker positions. The hammer on the right has a better marker position as it does not cause any hindrance to its natural usage. The marker placement on the left hammer, might cause hindrance in its natural way of usage in some cases.


  • In the figure below, (a) shows that the measuring tape's markers isn't visible to the camera when it is at rest. This will cause the marker to go undetected and hence return erroneous usage times. Similarly, in (b) the tools are cluttered and are placed on top of each other. This causes one or more markers going undetected. This will again result in wrong tool usage statistics. (c) shows the right way of placing tools at rest. All the markers are clearly visible to the camera at all times in the resting position.


  • Using the right size of the marker is also important. In the image below, in (a), we two markers being used. The left marker affixed onto the plier is very small and hence goes undetected as can be seen in (b). While the marker affixed to the hammer is of good size and is hence detected.


  • Avoid using low resolution markers or cameras. It can be seen from the image below in (a), that the marker goes undetected because of the blurriness induced due to low resolutions, while it is not the case in (b).


  • Avoid sticking the markers in the areas where the tools are held. There are three problems with this:

    • First, there is the case of the marker being obscured from the camera when its held, leading to a failed detection.
    • Second, this might damage the marker and hence might result in the marker being undetected.
    • Lastly, if the user tries to make sure the marker isn’t damaged he/she is likely to consciously focus on doing this. This changes the way the user is using the tool. The user’s attention and focus are now diverted away from the DIY task towards making sure the marker isn’t damaged. This is a form of hindrance and breaks away from the natural way of doing a tool-based task. This defeats the aim of our solution to preserve the natural way of tool usage.



  • The location of the placement of the marker on this scissor is very poor and not a good choice as the marker will almost always cause hindrances in the natural way of using the scissors, which isn't desired.
